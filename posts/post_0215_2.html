Two-Dimensional Wave Equation

Introduction

The two-dimensional wave equation extends the one-dimensional wave equation to describe wave propagation in a plane. It is widely used in acoustics, optics, and fluid dynamics.

Mathematical Formulation

The two-dimensional wave equation is given by:

\[ \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \left( \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} \right) \]

where:

  • \( u(x,y,t) \) represents the wave function at position \( (x,y) \) and time \( t \).
  • \( c \) is the wave speed.
  • \( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2} \), \( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2} \), and \( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2} \) are second-order partial derivatives.

General Solution

The general solution depends on boundary conditions and can be expressed using separation of variables:

\[ u(x,y,t) = X(x) Y(y) T(t) \]

which leads to a set of ordinary differential equations for \( X(x) \), \( Y(y) \), and \( T(t) \).

Conclusion

The two-dimensional wave equation is crucial for understanding wave dynamics in multiple fields such as sound propagation, fluid waves, and even quantum mechanics.
