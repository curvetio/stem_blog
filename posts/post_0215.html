One-Dimensional Wave Equation

Introduction

The one-dimensional wave equation describes how waves propagate in a given medium. It is a fundamental equation in physics and engineering.

Mathematical Formulation

The one-dimensional wave equation is given by:

\[ \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2 \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \]

where:

  • \( u(x,t) \) represents the wave function, which describes the displacement at position \( x \) and time \( t \).
  • \( c \) is the wave speed.
  • \( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial t^2} \) and \( \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2} \) are second-order partial derivatives.

General Solution

The general solution to the wave equation is given by the superposition of two traveling waves:

\[ u(x,t) = f(x - ct) + g(x + ct) \]

where \( f \) and \( g \) are arbitrary functions representing waves traveling in opposite directions.

Conclusion

The one-dimensional wave equation is widely used in physics to describe waves in strings, sound waves, and even electromagnetic waves.
